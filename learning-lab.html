The cognitive science behind why you read things and forget them — and what actually works instead.

Mental models tell you what to think about. These tell you how your brain actually works. Eleven concepts, mostly buried in academic papers, each one rewiring something you assumed was true.

The single principle behind all eleven

Easy learning is fake learning. The brain encodes deeply only when it has to struggle, generate, connect, and care. Every concept below is a different way of engineering that struggle into the act of reading.

Why this exists

I read constantly. I remember almost nothing. Every concept I skim slides off within a week.

Talking to friends, the same pattern. Smart, curious people, ploughing through books and articles, retaining a fraction. The conventional advice — re-read, highlight, take notes — turns out to mostly not work.

So I went looking for what does. The findings I've collected here aren't the famous productivity techniques. They're the foundational discoveries from cognitive psychology, mostly published in academic journals, mostly invisible to mainstream readers.

Each one overturns something I previously assumed. Each one is supported by decades of replication. Each one is doable, today, by anyone.

This is a learning-in-public project. I'm building these tools to fix my own reading, sharing what I find as I go.
